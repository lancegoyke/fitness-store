Welcome to your program, Athlete!
How to perform breathing drills
1. All drills are to be performed with a relaxed, open mouth "sigh" on the exhale, not a forceful exhale, so as to not over-activate superficial abdominals.2. Hold for 4-5s after each exhale before taking a quiet inhale through the nose3. DO NOT allow lower ribs to flare up upon inhalation. Air should fill up the chest and upper back.
How to approach movementReview this blog post for details
Warm up sets Perform as many warm up sets as needed to prepare for working sets, without creating unnecessary fatigue.
Example warm upWorking weight=315 x 10. Warm up sets: 45lbs x 8-10, 135 x 5-8, 225 x 3-5, 275 x 1-2, 295 x 1
RPE/RIR RPE=Rating Perceived Exertion. 1-10 scale. This will correlate with RIR (reps in reserve). A 9RPE=1RIR, meaning you could probably could have done 1 more rep. 8RPE=2 more reps, etc.
Selecting weightsFind a weight that = 8ish RPE on first set. Should be able to maintain 9-9.5 RPE for all sets. Can push towards 10 RPE on last set on wks 3-4. Keep in mind we will be keeping the same exercises for multiple blocks, so select weights that allow future progression
How double progression works3 x 12-15 example: Get 12,12,12 Week 1. Goal would be at least 13,12,12 on Week 2. Keep increasing reps until you hit 15,15,15 then increase weight and begin progression again.
Supersets and circuitsSupersets and circuits are indicated by a number followed by a latter. Ex: "5a" is paired with "5b." If 3 sets are prescribed, you will perform one set of 5a, followed by one set of 5b, then repeat twice.
What are these exercises?
If you're unsure on how to perform an exercise, I have a running list of links to video demonstrations and explanations on one of my websites: https://mastering.fitness/exercises/. Let me know if you can't find what you're looking for!
Timer If you're doing intervals, feel free to use my timer: https://mastering.fitness/timer/
Checking your formIf a movement doesn't feel correct or if it's painful, I would love to see a video of it.Record a side and front view video, upload as "Unlisted" to YouTube, and send me the link.
Sharing is caringPlease share your journey through the template on social media! Feel free to tag @lancegoyke in any posts. I absolutely love hearing your feedback, and are excited to see how you progress!
